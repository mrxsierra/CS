# Referral and Networking Strategies

In 2026, over 70% of high-paying tech roles are filled via referrals before the job is ever posted on a public board. Networking is not about "asking for favors," it's about building professional relationships.

---

## 1. The Power of Referrals
A referral usually guarantees a recruiter screen, bypassing the initial ATS filter.
- **Internal Referrals**: From employees within the company.
- **External Referrals**: From investors, customers, or partners of the company.

## 2. Optimized LinkedIn Networking
- **Profile as a Landing Page**: Ensure your headline is searchable (e.g., "Senior Backend Engineer | Python | Distributed Systems").
- **The "Warm" Reach Out**: Connect with former colleagues, alumni, or people you've met at conferences.
- **The "Cold" Reach Out**: When reaching out to a stranger, focus on curiosity, not the job.
    - *Bad*: "Can you refer me to [Link]?"
    - *Good*: "I saw your team is working on [Project]. As someone interested in [Technology], I'd love to learn about how you handle [Specific Challenge] if you have 15 minutes."

## 3. The Informational Interview
The goal is to gather intelligence and build rapport.
- **Questions to Ask**:
    - "What is the biggest challenge your team is currently facing?"
    - "How is the engineering culture different here compared to your previous roles?"
    - "What qualities does the team value most during the interview process?"

## 4. How to Ask for the Referral
Once you've established a connection or have a warm lead:
- **Be Concise**: State the exact job ID or link.
- **Provide a Blurb**: Give your referrer a 3-sentence summary they can copy-paste into the referral system.
    - *Example*: "I've worked with [Name] on [Project]. They have deep expertise in [Skill] and would be a great fit for [Role] because of [Specific Achievement]."

---

## Actionable Checklist
- [ ] List 20 companies you'd love to work for.
- [ ] Identify 1-2 people at each company (Alumni, 2nd-degree connections).
- [ ] Send 5 personalized connection requests per week.
- [ ] Prepare your "Referral Blurb."
