# FAQ & The Definitive Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

This guide addresses common doubts for the 2026-27 job market and provides a final checklist for your preparation.

## 1. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: I am from a Tier-3 non-technical college. Do I have a real chance against Tier-1 graduates?
**Yes.** While Tier-1 graduates have direct campus pipelines, the off-campus market is highly skill-driven. Companies in 2026-27 use live coding sandboxes to screen candidates. If your GitHub contains solid production code and you pass their technical assessments, they will hire you over a Tier-1 graduate who has only memorized algorithmic theory.

### Q2: I have a career gap of 2+ years. How do I explain this to recruiters?
**Do not hide it or apologize for it.** Reframe the gap as an engineering sprint:
- ✅ *Reframe*: "During this period, I executed a career pivot. I treated it as a full-time engineering sprint: I upskilled in modern system engineering, built and deployed production-grade projects, and worked on freelance automation scripts. You can inspect my commit history on my GitHub."

### Q3: Should I spend money on costly certifications (AWS, Scrum, etc.)?
For entry-level development, **no**. Hiring managers care about whether you can write clean, working code. The only exception is for specialized DevOps or Cloud Architect tracks, where a foundational AWS Associate certification can help validate basic infrastructure knowledge.

### Q4: Will AI completely replace software engineers by 2027?
**No.** AI tools are outstanding at generating code syntax, but they cannot read human business requirements accurately, design complex system architectures with deep trade-offs, or debug unpredictable integration issues. The engineers who will be replaced are the ones who refuse to learn how to use AI tools to speed up their development workflows.

## 2. The Definitive Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

| DO THIS ✅ | AVOID THIS ❌ |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Tailor your resume** for every Job Description you apply to. | Send the exact same resume to hundreds of job postings. |
| **Write comprehensive unit tests** and documentation for your projects. | Build generic tutorial projects like Todo Apps or clone UIs. |
| **Earn referrals** by building real relationships with other engineers. | Send demanding "Refer me" messages to strangers on LinkedIn. |
| **Master the underlying internals** of one specific stack deeply. | Learn multiple programming languages superficially at the same time. |
| **Prepare structured stories** using the STAR method for behavioral rounds. | Give long, unfocused answers during managerial and culture interviews. |
| **Audit AI-generated code** line-by-line before committing. | Use AI tools as a cognitive crutch without understanding the logic. |
