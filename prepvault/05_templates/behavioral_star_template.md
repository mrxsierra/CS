# ⭐ Behavioral STAR Story Map

> [!tip] The STAR Method is the universal language of behavioral interviews.
> Every answer must follow: **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult.
> Use this template to map one story per major behavioral category.

---

## Story Metadata

| Field | Your Notes |
|-------|------------|
| **Story Title** | `_Give your story a memorable name_` |
| **Category** | `☐ Conflict  ☐ Leadership  ☐ Initiative  ☐ Technical Challenge` |
| **Target Companies** | `_e.g., Amazon (Ownership), Google (Googleyness)_` |
| **Leadership Principles Mapped** | `_e.g., Customer Obsession, Bias for Action_` |

## 📖 1. Situation — Set the Scene

> *Describe the context. When and where did this happen? Who was involved?*

**Company / Team**: `_e.g., Acme Corp, Platform Engineering team (8 engineers)_`

**Background**: 
```
What was the environment, project, or product?
What was at stake?
```

**Key Constraints**:
- `_Time pressure? Budget? Headcount? Legacy system? Regulatory? _`

---

## 🎯 2. Task — What Needed to Be Done

> *What was your specific responsibility? What goal were you working toward?*

**Your Role**: `_Were you leading, contributing, coordinating?_`

**The Objective**:
```
What needed to be accomplished — and why was it important?
```

**Difficulty Level**: `☐ Easy  ☐ Moderate  ☐ Hard  ☐ Very Hard`

---

## ⚡ 3. Action — What You Actually Did

> *This is the longest and most detailed section. Focus on **your** contributions, not the team's.*

### Step-by-Step Actions

| # | What I Did | Why This Approach | Skills Demonstrated |
|---|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | `_` | `_` | `_e.g., Root cause analysis_` |
| 2 | `_` | `_` | `_e.g., Cross-team collaboration_` |
| 3 | `_` | `_` | `_e.g., Technical architecture decision_` |
| 4 | `_` | `_` | `_e.g., Stakeholder communication_` |

### Key Decisions Made
- **Decision**: `_What tradeoff did you face?_`
- **Alternatives Considered**: `_Option A vs. Option B_`
- **Why Selected**: `_Reasoning and data used_`

### Challenges Overcome
- `_Technical obstacle and how you solved it_`
- `_Interpersonal obstacle and how you resolved it_`

---

## 📊 4. Result — Quantify the Impact

> *Concrete, measurable outcomes. Numbers speak louder than words.*

### Measurable Metrics

| Metric | Before | After | Delta |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| `_e.g., Page load time_` | `_800ms_` | `_200ms_` | `✅ 75% improvement` |
| `_e.g., Team velocity_` | `_20 pts/sprint_` | `_35 pts/sprint_` | `✅ 75% increase` |
| `_e.g., Customer tickets_` | `_200/week_` | `_50/week_` | `✅ 75% reduction` |

### Recognition & Learning
- **Acknowledgment**: `_Promotion? Award? Shout-out from leadership?_`
- **Key Takeaway**: `_What did you learn that you've applied since?_`
- **Reusable Artifact**: `_Did you create a playbook, tool, or process that others still use?_`

## 🧠 Reflection & Interview Prep

### How This Story Maps to Common Questions

| Question Type | How This Story Fits |
|---------------|---------------------|
| "Tell me about a conflict" | `_Adjust framing to emphasize the disagreement_` |
| "Describe a leadership moment" | `_Adjust framing to emphasize influence without authority_` |
| "Tell me about a failure" | `_Adjust framing to emphasize learning (Result = lesson)_` |
| "Describe a technical challenge" | `_Emphasize the complexity and your analysis_` |

### Alternative Endings (for "failure" variants)
- **What I would do differently**: `_Honest reflection showing growth_`
- **What went wrong**: `_Root cause you hadn't considered_`

## 📋 Completed Story Checklist

- [ ] Situation is concise (3-4 sentences)
- [ ] Task is clearly my responsibility (not the team's)
- [ ] Action has 3+ concrete steps with reasoning
- [ ] Result has at least one quantified metric
- [ ] Story time ≤ 90 seconds when spoken
- [ ] Maps to 2+ Leadership Principles
- [ ] Can be adapted to 3+ different question framings
