# Job Hunt Survival Guide: Mindset & Wellness

The technical interview process is a marathon, not a sprint. Maintaining your mental health is as important as mastering DP.

---

## 1. Managing the "Rejected" Mindset
Rejection is a data point, not a verdict on your worth as an engineer.
- **The 24-Hour Rule**: Give yourself 24 hours to feel the frustration of a rejection, then move into "Post-Mortem" mode.
- **Focus on the Process**: You can control your preparation; you cannot control the interviewer's mood or other candidates.

## 2. Preventing Preparation Burnout
- **Pomodoro Technique**: 50 minutes of deep work, 10 minutes of total disconnection.
- **Digital Detox**: At least 2 hours before bed, stay off screens to ensure deep sleep (critical for memory retention).
- **The "One Win" Day**: Even on bad days, aim for one small win (e.g., "I understood one new concept today").

## 3. Physical Readiness
- **Sleep**: 7-8 hours is non-negotiable for cognitive performance.
- **Hydration**: Drink water throughout the interview day.
- **The "Power Pose"**: 2 minutes of standing confidently before an interview can lower cortisol and increase testosterone/confidence.

## 4. Building a Support System
- **Study Groups**: Join a Discord or local group of people in the same process.
- **Mental Health Professional**: If the stress becomes overwhelming, don't hesitate to seek professional help.
